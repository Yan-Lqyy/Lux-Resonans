Conducting the Experiment

Apparatus Needed:

  • Light Source:
    • A laser (e.g., red He-Ne laser or a laser pointer) is ideal as it's monochromatic and coherent.
    • Alternatively, a bright lamp with a narrow color filter and a pre-slit (to create a coherent line source) can be used, though the pattern may be less sharp.
  • Slits:
    • Commercially available slides with single and double slits of known widths and separations.
    • DIY slits can be made carefully using razor blades or by painting a microscope slide black and scratching fine lines.
  • Screen: A white piece of paper, a wall, or a translucent screen.
  • Measuring Tools: A ruler or calipers to measure distances (slit-to-screen, fringe spacing). An optical bench can help with alignment and measurement.

Basic Setup:

  1. Alignment: Position the light source, slit(s), and screen in a straight line. The light should be incident normally (perpendicularly) on the slit assembly.

    Diagram of a typical double-slit experiment setup. (Source: Wikimedia Commons)

  2. Dark Environment: Conduct the experiment in a darkened room to make the faint fringes more visible.
  3. Distances:
    • The distance 'L' from the slits to the screen should be significantly larger than the slit separation 'd' and slit width 'a' (e.g., L ~ 1 meter, d ~ 0.1-0.5 mm, a ~ 0.04-0.1 mm).
    • The slit separation 'd' should typically be a few times larger than the slit width 'a'.

Procedure:

  1. Shine the light source through the chosen slit (single or double).
  2. Observe the pattern of bright and dark fringes on the screen.
  3. Measurements (Example for determining wavelength):
    • Measure the distance 'L' from the slits to the screen.
    • For double slits, measure the distance 'x' between several bright fringes (e.g., across 5 fringes). Calculate the average fringe spacing Δx = x / (number of fringes - 1).
    • Use the formula for bright fringes in a double-slit experiment (for small angles): Δx = (λL) / d.
    • If 'd' (slit separation) and 'L' are known, you can calculate the wavelength λ: λ = (Δx * d) / L.
    • Similar measurements and calculations can be done for single-slit diffraction to determine slit width 'a' or wavelength.
  4. Observe how the pattern changes if you:
    • Change the color of light (wavelength).
    • Change the slit width 'a'.
    • Change the slit separation 'd' (for double slits).
    • Change the distance 'L' to the screen.

Safety Precautions:

  • Laser Safety: NEVER look directly into a laser beam or its strong reflections. Do not point a laser at other people. If using a Class 3R/3B or higher laser, appropriate laser safety goggles should be worn. Be aware of reflective surfaces.
  • General lab safety if using other electrical equipment.
